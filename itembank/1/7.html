Item 159: Name/ID: Operations and Algebraic Thinking 1.OA.4 / 1.OA.4
Proctor Instructions: Make sure all students are ready for the question.
 
Say: Ramona has twelve eggs. She used three of them to make an omelet. She used the equation 12 - 3 = ? to find out how many eggs she has left. Which think-aloud did Ramona use to solve her problem?
 
Say: Start with 12. There are 8 more needed to make 20. Then take 3 away to make 17. There are 17 eggs left: 12 + 5 = 17; start with 12. There are 3 more needed to make 15. There are 15 eggs left: 12 + 3 = 15; start with 3. There are 8 more needed to make 10. There are 2 more needed to make 12. That is 8 and 2, so there are 10 eggs left: 12 - 3 = 10; or Start with 3. There are 7 more needed to make 10. There are 2 more needed to make 12. That is 7 and 2, so there are 9 eggs left: 12 - 3 = 9. Choose the correct answer.
 
 A.
Start with 12. There are 8 more needed to make 20. Then take 3 away to make 17. There are 17 eggs left: 12 + 5 = 17.
 B.
Start with 12. There are 3 more needed to make 15. There are 15 eggs left: 12 + 3 = 15.
 C.
Start with 3. There are 8 more needed to make 10. There are 2 more needed to make 12. That is 8 and 2, so there are 10 eggs left: 12 - 3 = 10.
D.
Start with 3. There are 7 more needed to make 10. There are 2 more needed to make 12. That is 7 and 2, so there are 9 eggs left: 12 - 3 = 9.
