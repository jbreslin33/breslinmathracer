Item 226: Name/ID: Statistics and Probability 6.SP.5.d / 6.SP.5.d Item 02
Micah sees a list of odd jobs and payment amounts on a bulletin board.
 

 
Which measure of variability would be the best if Micah is interested in the spread of the data?
 
A.
The mean absolute deviation is best because it measures average distance from the mean.
 B.
The interquartile range is best because it divides the amounts into four groups.
 C.
The striking deviations are the best because they show any amounts that are not close to the average.
 D.
None of these measures would best help Micah analyze the spread of the data.

Item 228: Name/ID: Statistics and Probability 6.SP.5.d / 6.SP.5.d Item 04
After a scavenger hunt, Axel and Brady survey their friends to see how many items were found.
 
 
Together they find the mean number of items found by each child to be 12.
 
Both boys want to look at the spread of this data. Axel believes using interquartile ranges will be the best way to do this. Brady believes finding the mean absolute deviation is the best way to do this.
 
A. Determine which boy is correct, and use his method to determine the value that will allow them to study the spread of the data.
 
B. Interpret the result from part A, and make a generalization about the spread of the data and what it means in relation to this scenario.
